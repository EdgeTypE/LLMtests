Methodology

The Science
of Deduction

Holmes operates not on intuition but on rigorous observation — every fibre of clothing, every callus on a hand, every trace of foreign soil speaks a language to those trained to listen.

"When you have eliminated the impossible, whatever remains, however improbable, must be the truth."

  • 01
    Observation
    The disciplined, total cataloguing of all available physical evidence before a single hypothesis is formed.
  • 02
    Deduction
    Linking observed data through logical chains, eliminating all impossible interpretations until only truth remains.
  • 03
    Knowledge Base
    Chemistry, anatomy, London criminal history, tobacco ash, soil types, cipher codes — a vast and precisely organised mind.
  • 04
    Disguise & Theatre
    Holmes deploys persona and performance as investigative tools, operating unseen beneath countless identities.
  • 05
    Network
    The Irregulars, Mycroft, and a web of informants extend Holmes's reach into every corner of the city.

The Baker Street Gazette
Dispatches from 221B · Est. 1881 · Price: One Penny

The Consulting Detective Speaks

It is a capital mistake to theorise before one has data. Insensibly one begins to twist facts to suit theories, instead of theories to suit facts — a habit which leads the reasoner, invariably, into error.

"You see, but you do not observe. The distinction is clear."

Holmes received us in his Baker Street rooms, a pipe of shag tobacco beside him, several volumes of criminal reference scattered across the chemical-stained mantelpiece. He appeared, as always, in a state of intense repose — the coiled spring before its release.

Profile

Height: over six feet. Eyes: sharp, piercing grey. Born 6 January 1854, possibly in Yorkshire. Practised for twenty-three years from Baker Street before retirement to a small Sussex farm and the keeping of bees.

His monographs include Upon the Distinction Between the Ashes of the Various Tobaccos and The Book of Life, published in the Strand.

Baker Street

The famous lodgings at 221B: a first-floor sitting room, two bedrooms above, the landlady Mrs. Hudson below. The mantelpiece bristles with correspondence pinned by a jack-knife.

Visitors have noted: a bear-skin rug, a Persian slipper housing tobacco, and a chemical laboratory of considerable disorder.
